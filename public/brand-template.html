Answer every question as honestly and specifically as possible. Vague answers produce generic content. Specific answers — real numbers, real stories, real client language — are what allow the AI to write in your exact voice and produce posts that feel genuinely yours. There are no wrong answers. There is only more detail or less detail.

01

Identity Foundation

Why you exist, what you stand for, where you're going

Matt Gray — Founder OS Dan Martell — Authority Engine
01
What is your brand in one powerful sentence?
Not a tagline — a positioning claim. What do you do, for whom, and what becomes possible because of it?
e.g. "I help ambitious founders build a personal brand so strong that clients find them — without cold outreach."
02
What are your 3–5 non-negotiable core values?
These are the filters for what you say yes to publicly. If your content, partnerships, or offers violate these, it feels wrong — to you and to your audience.
03
What is your 1–3 year vision?
Not a mission statement. The concrete future state you're publicly pulling toward — what your life, business, or impact looks like when you've arrived.
e.g. "In 3 years I run a $5M/year one-person business, working 4 hours a day, from wherever I choose — and I've helped 500 founders do the same."
04
How do your best clients or followers naturally describe you?
Not how you describe yourself — how do THEY describe what you do when recommending you? Quote real testimonials or DMs if you have them.
e.g. "The person who finally made LinkedIn make sense for me" / "A straight-talking strategist who actually builds things, not just talks"
05
What is the one thing you want to be the most known person in the world for?
Your north star claim. If you could be famous for one thing among the people who matter to you, what would it be?
02

Origin Story & Authority

The proof that earns you the right to teach or sell

Hormozi — Proof Stack Martell — Contrast Arc Gray — Origin Story
06
What is your origin story?
The narrative arc that gives you the right to teach or lead in this space. The before (where you were), the turning point (what happened), and the discovery (what changed everything). Be specific — name the moment, the decision, the result.
07
What is your evidence stack?
Your 5 most specific proof points. Exact numbers, exact results, exact timelines. Generic claims ("I've helped hundreds of clients") produce generic content. Specific proof ("47 founders hit their first $10K month in under 90 days") is what the AI can use.
08
What is your Contrast Arc?
A belief you held that turned out to be completely wrong — and what you discovered instead. This is one of the most powerful content structures because it teaches through your growth, not your expertise.
e.g. "I used to believe that more followers = more revenue. Then I lost 80% of my following in an algorithm change and made my best month ever. I discovered that email subscribers at 1,000 are worth more than Instagram followers at 50,000."
I used to think
Then I realized
Which revealed
09
What have you done that most people in your industry would consider impossible or surprising?
The achievement that makes people raise an eyebrow when they hear it. This becomes a "pattern interrupt" in your content — it breaks autopilot scrolling.
10
What do you know now that you wish someone had told you 5 years ago?
The shortcut, the lesson, the mindset shift that would have saved you time, money, or pain. This is pure content gold — it positions you as a guide, not a lecturer.
03

Market & Avatar

Who you serve and what they're really going through

Hormozi — Pain / Dream / Vehicle Priestley — Campaign Micro-Niche
11
Who is your exact buyer?
Specific role, company stage, situation. Not "business owners" — describe the specific person at a specific moment in their journey.
e.g. "B2B SaaS founders doing $300K–$2M ARR, who have a product that works but have no idea how to market it — they've tried paid ads, hired a content agency, and neither moved the needle."
12
What is the daily pain they carry — in their own words?
The inner monologue. What do they say to themselves at 2am when they can't sleep? Not what you think they feel — what they actually say. Use their language, not yours.
e.g. "Why is my competitor with a worse product getting all the attention? I'm doing everything right and nothing is working. Maybe I'm just not good at this."
13
What is their specific dream outcome?
The emotional end state — not the feature or deliverable. Not "more leads" — what does having more leads actually give them? What does life look and feel like on the other side?
e.g. "They want to be the person in their industry who gets invited to speak, who gets inbound opportunities they don't have to chase, who other founders mention when asked who to follow."
14
What is the vehicle they believe in?
The method or mechanism your buyer thinks will get them to their dream outcome. What's the approach they've already bought into? Aligning to this reduces resistance.
15
What is the buying trigger?
The specific moment or event that finally pushes them to take action. What tips a fence-sitter into a buyer?
e.g. "They just watched a competitor land a Forbes feature and realized visibility is the lever they're missing."
16
What have they already tried that didn't work? Why did it fail?
This is critical for positioning. If you understand why their previous attempts failed, you can position your approach as the thing that finally works — and show you understand their frustration.
17
Does your market have these four qualities? Describe each briefly.
Hormozi's market selection criteria. If any of the four are weak, that's a positioning problem worth naming.

Purchasing Power

Positioning

How you own a specific lane in the market

Priestley — The Pitch Hormozi — Niche Formula Martell — Positioning Claim
18
Complete Priestley's Pitch: "I work with ___ to solve ___ so that they can ___."
The test: does it make someone ask "How do you do that?" If they can nod and move on, it's too broad. Keep tightening until it generates curiosity.

19
What is your micro-niche?
Narrow enough to eliminate all direct competition. Wide enough to build a business. The micro-niche is the intersection of your buyer, their stage, and your unique method.
e.g. "LinkedIn content strategy for bootstrapped B2B SaaS founders at $500K–$5M ARR"
20
What do you do differently or better than anyone else in your space?
Not features — method, philosophy, or approach. The thing that makes your clients say "I've never seen this done this way before."
21
What is your contrarian belief?
An opinion about your industry that most people in it would disagree with. The hill you're willing to die on. This is the engine of thought leadership — if everyone agrees with you, you're not saying anything.
e.g. "The entire personal branding industry is selling vanity metrics. Posting daily doesn't build a business. Building one undeniable offer and talking about it obsessively for 12 months does."
22
What do you explicitly refuse to be associated with?
What you are NOT, what you won't do, who you won't work with, what tactics you reject. Saying no clearly is what makes your yes credible.
23
What is the name of your methodology, framework, or signature approach?
A named system is more valuable than unnamed expertise. "The Buyback Loop," "The Content GPS," "The Grand Slam Offer" — these names create intellectual property from raw knowledge. If you don't have one yet, describe the steps and we'll name it.

07

Brand Associations & Visibility

Who knows you, who you partner with, what your name means

Priestley — 5 Ps: Profile & Partnerships Hormozi — Association Engineering
37
List 10 associations you want your name to carry.
Words, feelings, ideas, people — what should come to mind when someone hears your name? What reputation are you building?
38
Who are the right 1,000 people who need to know you exist?
Not 1 million — the specific 1,000 buyers, partners, and referrers who, if they knew you well, would change everything. Where do they congregate? Specific events, communities, Slack groups, subreddits, podcasts.
39
List 5–10 dream appearances, stages, or media placements.
Specific podcasts, events, publications, or newsletters where your exact buyer concentrates. Being on these is a shortcut to Priestley's "selective fame" — known by the right people, not everyone.
40
Who are 10 non-competing professionals who serve your exact buyer?
Potential partnership targets. They solve a different problem for the same person. A warm intro from them is worth 1,000 cold posts. Name real people or companies.
41
Who are your Dream 100?
The influencers, thought leaders, and collaborators in your space whose audience overlaps with yours. Not who you idolize — who shares your exact buyer and whose endorsement or collaboration would accelerate your brand. List the ones that come to mind first.
42
What is your brand personality in 4 adjectives?
If your brand were a person walking into a room, how would they be described? These become the guardrails for every piece of content.
43
